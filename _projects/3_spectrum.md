At the physical layer, spectrum sensing is detection theory. At **layer 2**, it becomes something SUTRA knows well: cooperative sensing coordination, MAC scheduling, and dynamic spectrum access — i.e., *allocating and coordinating a shared resource across distributed nodes under uncertainty*.

We approach 6G spectrum as a resource to be priced, allocated, and learned, reusing the toolkit from our market- and auction-based edge resource allocation {% cite prasad2017raera prasad2019dmc prasad2019ofm %} and reinforcement-learning-based management {% cite hegde2023counsel %}. Cooperative, privacy-preserving sensing across nodes ties naturally to our federated-learning work {% cite dey2026greenedge %}.

**Open directions:** cooperative sensing fusion, spectrum markets, and learned access policies for dense heterogeneous 6G nodes.
