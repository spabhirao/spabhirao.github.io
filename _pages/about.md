I am an Assistant Professor in the [Mehta Family School of Data Science and AI](https://iitpkd.ac.in/) at the **Indian Institute of Technology Palakkad**, where I lead the **SUTRA Lab**. My research employs mathematical and machine-learning methods to solve systems problems in emerging cloud-networking technologies — spanning **systems ML, algorithmic game theory, the edge–space–cloud continuum, and 6G**.

Over the past decade in industry and academia I have worked on how distributed computing resources should be *sensed, represented, priced, allocated, and orchestrated* under uncertainty. That thread runs from mechanism design and market-based resource procurement in the cloud, through robust configuration of complex services, to today's learning-driven orchestration of serverless and edge platforms. I earned my Ph.D. from the **University of Göttingen**, Germany, as a Marie Skłodowska-Curie fellow in the CleanSky ITN. 

**SUTRA** — *sūtra*, the Sanskrit word for a **thread** that binds and an **aphorism** that distills — captures how the lab weaves heterogeneous, distributed resources into a coherent, trustworthy fabric. 

I am always looking for motivated students interested in networked systems, ML for systems, and the theory of resource allocation. If that sounds like you, please reach out by email.
