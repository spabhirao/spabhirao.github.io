Layer-2 Spectrum Sensing for 6G

Cooperative MAC-layer spectrum sensing and dynamic spectrum access as a distributed resource-allocation and learning problem.

At the physical layer, spectrum sensing is detection theory. At layer 2, it becomes something SUTRA knows well: cooperative sensing coordination, MAC scheduling, and dynamic spectrum access — i.e., allocating and coordinating a shared resource across distributed nodes under uncertainty.

We approach 6G spectrum as a resource to be priced, allocated, and learned, reusing the toolkit from our market- and auction-based edge resource allocation (Prasad et al., 2017; Prasad et al., 2019; Prasad et al., 2019) and reinforcement-learning-based management (Hegde et al., 2023). Cooperative, privacy-preserving sensing across nodes ties naturally to our federated-learning work (Dey et al., 2026).

Open directions: cooperative sensing fusion, spectrum markets, and learned access policies for dense heterogeneous 6G nodes.
